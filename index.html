How I work 03

My Design Process

Good design doesn't happen by accident. I follow a structured, user-centered process that balances creative thinking with analytical rigor — from the first research question to the final pixel.

01 — Discover
Research & Empathize
I start by understanding the problem space — who are the users, what are their needs, and what context do they operate in? I use interviews, competitive analysis, and desk research to build a solid foundation.
User Interviews Desk Research Competitive Analysis
02 — Define
Synthesize & Frame
Raw insights get structured into clear problem statements, personas, and user journey maps. I identify patterns and define the "how might we" questions that guide the rest of the process.
Personas User Journeys Affinity Mapping
03 — Ideate
Concepts & Sketches
With the problem clearly framed, I explore a wide range of solutions. Sketching fast, thinking divergently — quantity over quality at this stage. No idea is too wild to explore.
Sketching Moodboards Brainstorming
04 — Design
Wireframes & UI
The best ideas get refined into wireframes, then high-fidelity designs. I build scalable design systems with consistent components, clear hierarchy, and pixel-perfect visual quality.
Figma Wireframes Design Systems
05 — Test
Prototype & Validate
Interactive prototypes get tested with real users. I observe, listen, and collect feedback — then iterate. Design is never done after the first round; it improves through honest testing.
Prototyping Usability Tests Iteration
06 — Deliver
Handoff & Launch
Clean handoff to development with annotated specs, assets, and design tokens. I stay involved during implementation to ensure the final product matches the intended experience.
Dev Handoff Documentation Quality Check
